About Us

Soil pH is a measure of the acidity or alkalinity of the soil. It is an important factor in determining the suitability of the soil for plant growth. Most plants prefer a slightly acidic to neutral soil pH, ranging from 6.0 to 7.0. However, some plants like blueberries and azaleas require a more acidic soil pH, while others like alfalfa and clover require a more alkaline soil pH.

The importance of soil pH cannot be overstated. A soil pH that is too high or too low can affect the availability of nutrients to plants, leading to reduced growth and yields. For example, a soil pH that is too high can lead to a deficiency of nutrients like iron and manganese, while a soil pH that is too low can lead to a toxicity of nutrients like aluminum and manganese.

Fertilizer recommendation is the practice of determining the type and amount of fertilizer to apply to a crop based on the soil pH and other factors like soil type, climate, and crop type. It is an important aspect of crop production, as it can help to improve crop yields and reduce the environmental impact of farming. A well-planned fertilizer recommendation can help to reduce the amount of fertilizer applied, minimize waste, and prevent pollution.
